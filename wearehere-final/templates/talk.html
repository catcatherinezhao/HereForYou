I want to talk to someone.

Thank you for sharing and making a report. We’d like to show you the multitude of options you have available at Dartmouth for talking to someone and getting support.

SAPAs

  • sapa@dartmouth.edu
    •  
  • A group of trained Dartmouth Students invested in providing, informed, empathic,
  • and empowerment-based support to Dartmouth students impacted by sexual
  • and gender-based violence.

Sexperts

  • Sexperts@dartmouth.edu
  • Advisor: Tong Fei, Healthy Relationships and Sexual Health Specialist
  • Student Wellness Center Robinson (Robo) Hall, Suite 319
  • (603) 646-9414
    •  
  • Sexual Health Peer Educators serve as campus educational peer educators and provide
  • advising services for students who wish to speak to a peer on an individual basis.

Dick's House Counselors and Doctors

  • Regular business hours for counselors: (603) 646-9442
  • After business hours for counselors: (603) 646-9440
    •  
  • The clinicians are trained on issues surrounding sexual violence and can provide support
  • and information to help you process your experience, guide you in coping with the trauma,
  • and help you consider options and decide what will be most beneficial to healing.
  • They can offer support and information to help you make your own informed, empowered decisions.

Tucker Center Chaplain

  • Daveen.H.Litwin@dartmouth.edu
  • (603) 646-3780
    •  
  • Rabbi Daveen Litwin is the dean and chaplain of the Tucker Center on campus.
  • If you would like to speak to other religious support individuals, please see the Tucker Center website.

WISE

  • 24 hr crisis line: (603) 448-5525
  • (603) 448-5922
  • (866) 348-WISE
  • delaney@wiseuv.org
    •  
  • WISE advocates are also available on campus Mondays from 8 am-4 pm in 37 Dewey Field Road, room 452.
  • WISE leads the Upper Valley to end gender-based violence through survivor-centered advocacy,
  • prevention, education and mobilization for social change.
